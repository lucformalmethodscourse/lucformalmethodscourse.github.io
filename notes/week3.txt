Exploring Black Box and White Box Testing
Konstantin discussed the concept of black box testing and white box testing, explaining that black box testing involves testing without knowledge of the implementation, while white box testing involves designing tests in conjunction with knowledge of the implementation. He used the example of a sort method in a library to illustrate how white box testing could help identify potential issues, such as the use of different algorithms for smaller and larger arrays. Konstantin also explored the idea of boundary values in testing, using the example of a padding method in a library. He suggested that boundary values could be identified by testing around the threshold of the method's behavior, and that these values could be used to refine the understanding of the method's arguments.

Property-Based Testing Explained
Konstantin discussed the concept of property-based testing, focusing on its universality and exhaustiveness. He explained that property-based testing is more exhaustive than table-based testing, as it covers an infinite number of cases, whereas table-based testing covers a finite number of cases. He also mentioned that property-based testing is more concise than example-based testing. Konstantin further explained that in practice, property-based testing is implemented using automatic generation of argument combinations, which generates a large table automatically. He also discussed the importance of testing the logic of the system under test, using the example of a palindrome checker.

Stateless vs Stateful Testing Explained
Konstantin discussed the concept of stateless and stateful testing, explaining that stateless testing is based on the idea that the result of an invocation depends only on the actual values of the arguments, without any internal history or state. He used the example of a shopping cart to illustrate a stateful scenario. He also mentioned that next week they would be studying state-based testing, which involves testing different types and sequences of interactions with a data structure.
